Game Summary

This game wouldn't have happened if Bill's Lieutenant hadn't managed to get Rob on the phone. The Auburn network had been shot to hell and none inside could get out. Fortunately, all outsiders could get in.

The lag was horrible. Bases popped like balloons, shipyards became radioactive in a matter of minutes. THP managed to use 4th-army skimming to build a lead and build and build. Some strategic errors on the part of FBM players sealed their fate.

The game almost ended in a genocide when THP got a repair base right next to the FBM last planet stand, but the planetary defenders were too thick. Final score was 23-1.

Post-Game Interview with the Captains

Archivist:
So, Hammor, how do you feel about winning 2 out of 3?
Hammor:
I'm happy about it.

We have shown that THP can take advantage of complete chaos better than anyone else.

Archivist:
How many bases did you lose?
Hammor:
Oh I guess it was about 5. Poor Bolo was getting 3-second pauses. Once he got smacked because he was repairing.
Archivist:
Do you think you'll fight any more matches against FBM?
Hammor:
Probably not this season, but I wouldn't bet money on it. Bill will probably be itching for revenge.
Archivist:
Thought, a penny for your (ahem) thoughts.
Thought:
I need to train my team. One of them tought that neuting planets was a good idea.
Archivist:
Hmm, still haven't solidified strategic doctrine?
Thought:
He never told anybody, he made it up himself.
Archivist:
Ah, a little creativity in the ranks :)
Thought:
In fact, I think I said not to beam planets below 3, period.
Archivist:
Ah, that sort of neuting.
Thought:
I need some time during play to teach, not just lead. It'd be nice if I had a roster full of old pros but there just aren't enough to go aruond.
Archivist:
Any last words, Thought?
Thought:
Yeah, just wait till next season.
